This principle emphasizes the importance of preserving the natural and cultural features of outdoor environments by minimizing human impact. This principle encourages outdoor enthusiasts to respect and protect the beauty and integrity of the places they visit.


Overview

Do Not Pick Plants or Disturb Wildlife. Refrain from picking flowers, plants, or any vegetation. Leave rocks, shells, and other natural features as you found them. Avoid disturbing wildlife by keeping a safe distance and not feeding or approaching animals too closely.

Stay on Established Trails and Paths. Stick to established trails and paths when hiking, biking, or horseback riding. Avoid creating new paths or cutting switchbacks, which can cause soil erosion and environmental damage.

Leave Natural and Cultural Features Alone. Avoid altering or disturbing natural and cultural features. This includes refraining from moving or rearranging rocks, leaving historical or cultural artifacts in place, and not defacing any structures or carvings.


Why is this important?

In many outdoor areas, there are cultural or historical sites of significance. Leaving what you find is crucial for preserving the historical value and cultural heritage of these sites.


What does this look like?

Scenario: A group of rock climbers visits a remote canyon to tackle challenging climbs. They make a point not to chip or alter the rock faces in any way to make their routes easier. Instead, they respect the natural rock formations and work within the existing challenges.

grade Importance: By leaving the rock formations untouched, the climbers ensure that future climbers can enjoy the same challenges and maintain the natural integrity of the canyon.
