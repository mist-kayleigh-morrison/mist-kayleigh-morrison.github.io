This principle aims to minimize human disturbance, maintain the safety of both wildlife and people, and protect the well-being of animals in their natural environment.


Overview

Observe from a Safe Distance. Observe animals from a safe distance. Do not feed them or approach them too closely.

Store Food Securely. Store food and trash securely to prevent wildlife from becoming accustomed to human food sources.

Keep Pets Under Control. If you have pets with you, keep them under control and on a leash. Uncontrolled pets can chase, harm, or stress wildlife.

Report Wildlife Harassment. If you witness others harassing wildlife or breaking wildlife protection laws, report it to the appropriate authorities or organizations.


Why is this important?

Wildlife relies on natural behaviors for survival and reproduction. Interfering with these behaviors, such as feeding or approaching animals too closely, can disrupt their daily routines and reproductive cycles. Respecting wildlife allows them to maintain their natural behaviors.


What does this look like?

Scenario: A family plans a hiking trip in an area with a known bear population. They educate themselves about bear safety, including the importance of not approaching or feeding bears. They carry bear-resistant food containers and follow proper food storage practices to prevent attracting bears to their campsite.

grade Importance: By respecting the natural behaviors and habitat of bears and avoiding close encounters, the family reduces the risk of dangerous human-bear interactions and helps protect both bears and themselves.
