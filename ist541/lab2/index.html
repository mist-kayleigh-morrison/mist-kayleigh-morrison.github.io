How to Pitch a Tent

By Kayleigh Morrison


Step 1: Pick a Place to Pitch

Before you start pitching your tent, check to make sure you have all the pieces and tools you'll need. Next you need to choose where to place it.

Gather your tent, poles, rainfly and tarp. Choose a spot to set up your tent. This should be as clear, level and flat as possible. Decide which way you want to orient your tent and lay down the tarp.


Step 2: Spread Out and Stake Your Tent

Stretch the tent base across the tarp. Pull the tent tight and stake two opposite corners to firm up the floor of your tent. Pull out the remaining corners and stake them as well.

error Tip: Pack a few extra stakes in case one breaks or you lose some!


Step 3: Connect the Poles

Your tent will either attach to the poles by sleeves or clips. Thread the poles through the sleeves or clip the material to the tent's frame, beginning in one corner before moving on to the opposite corner.

Make sure not to pull the poles through the sleeves- this might cause you to start over again.


Step 4: Set Up the Tent

Next, you'll need to raise the tent. Begin by elevating the tent and slowly raising one of the main tent poles. Each end of the pole should fit onto a faster pocket along with the exterior of your tent, closer to the ground. Repeat with the cross pole and additional support poles if it applies. Check for any additional fasteners or clips along side the poles that secure it to the outside of your tent.

If you have a rainfly and think you'll need it, drape it across the top of your tent. Look for fasteners on the outside of your tent to hold the fly in place.


Step 5: Add the Final Stakes and Supports

Finally, stake any remaining edges of your tent. Secure any ropes that may need staking to help keep the tent or rainfly firm. Once your tent is up, pull the poles as far apart as possible to make sure the material is nice and rigid. This not only gives you maximum room overhead, it also allows for more stability against potentially extreme weather.

Congratulations! You've successfully pitched a tent.
