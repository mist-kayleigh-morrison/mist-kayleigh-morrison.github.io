This principle focuses on minimizing the impact of outdoor activities on the environment by staying on established trails and using durable surfaces for camping.



Overview

Stay on Designated Trails. Stick to established trails and campsites to minimize impact.

Camp in Designated Campsites. Avoid creating new paths or campsites, especially in sensitive areas.

Stay Out of Sensitive Habitats. Keep out of sensitive vegetation such as wetlands, marshes, and fields of flowers. These areas are particularly vulnerable and can take years to recover from human impact.



Why is this important?

When people wander off designated trails or camp in undesignated areas, they can trample on fragile vegetation, disturb wildlife, and damage the ecosystem.

By sticking to established trails and campsites, we reduce the impact on these delicate environments, helping to preserve them for future generations.


What does this look like?

Scenario: A group of friends plans a hiking trip to a popular national park. As they explore the park's trails, they encounter some areas with sensitive vegetation. Instead of walking off-trail to take photos or explore, they stay on the established paths. This minimizes damage to the delicate plants and prevents soil erosion.

grade Importance: By staying on the designated trails, the hikers protect the fragile ecosystems within the national park, ensuring that future generations can also enjoy the pristine beauty of the area.
