About Leave No Trace (LNT)

The Leave No Trace (LNT) principles have a history dating back to the 1960s when outdoor enthusiasts, land management agencies, and conservation organizations began to address the increasing environmental impact of outdoor recreation. Over time, the principles evolved into the Leave No Trace Center for Outdoor Ethics, a nationally recognized organization dedicated to promoting responsible and sustainable outdoor ethics.

Today, the Leave No Trace Center for Outdoor Ethics remains committed to promoting responsible outdoor behavior, providing educational resources, and supporting land management agencies in their conservation efforts. The principles are considered a vital framework for protecting the natural world while ensuring that people can continue to enjoy the outdoors. Leave No Trace encourages a sense of stewardship and environmental responsibility among outdoor enthusiasts, helping to preserve the beauty and integrity of natural landscapes for future generations.


Learning Objectives:

1. List and explain the seven Leave No Trace principles.

2. Develop a sense of environmental stewardship.

3. Explain how to reduce the impact on the environment when recreating outdoors.

4. Explain that the choices we make can have positive or negative impacts on the environment and other people.
