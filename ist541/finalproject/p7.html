This principle emphasizes the importance of being respectful and courteous towards the broader outdoor community. It promotes harmonious and positive interactions among people, contributing to a more enjoyable and inclusive outdoor experience for all.


Overview

Yield the Trail. When hiking, biking, or on the trail, yield the right of way to other hikers or users as appropriate.

Minimize Noise. Keep noise levels to a minimum, respecting the serenity of the natural environment and the preferences of others.

Be Inclusive. Encourage inclusivity and diversity. Welcome people of all backgrounds, abilities, and ages to enjoy the outdoors alongside you. Share your knowledge and passion for the outdoors with others.

Share Public Spaces. In communal spaces, like picnic areas or viewpoints, be considerate and share the space with others. Avoid monopolizing areas and allow everyone to enjoy them.


Why is this important?

Being considerate of all visitors, regardless of their age, experience level, or background, promotes inclusivity and a welcoming outdoor environment. This allows people from diverse backgrounds to enjoy outdoor spaces comfortably.


What does this look like?

Scenario: On a popular hiking trail, hikers, mountain bikers, and equestrians all share the same path. Each group adheres to trail etiquette by yielding the right of way to others when appropriate.

grade Importance: This ensures a safe and courteous experience for all trail users.
