This principle focuses on the responsible disposal of waste, including trash, food scraps, human waste, and other debris. Proper waste disposal is essential for protecting the environment, preventing pollution, and maintaining the natural beauty of outdoor areas.



Overview

Pack It In, Pack It Out. Pack out all trash, litter, and food scraps. Leave nothing behind.

Pack Out Hygiene Products. Bury toilet paper deep in a cathole or pack the toilet paper out along with hygiene products.

Minimize Soap Usage. To wash yourself or your dishes, carry water 200 feet away from streams or lakes and use small amounts of biodegradable soap.

Properly Dispose of Human Waste. If no restroom facilities are available, you must follow specific guidelines for disposing of human waste. In many cases, this involves using a portable toilet or digging a "cathole" a certain distance from water sources and trails.

Why is this important?

Proper waste disposal helps protect natural ecosystems from pollution and degradation. When waste is managed responsibly, it reduces the negative impact on the environment, ensuring the preservation of pristine outdoor spaces.


What does this look like?

Scenario: A solo backpacker explores a remote wilderness area. To minimize their impact, they use a camp stove for cooking instead of having open fires. For washing dishes they choose to carry water away from water sources and use biodegradable soap sparingly.

grade Importance: This backpacker's responsible waste management helps preserve water quality, protect aquatic life, and reduce their ecological impact.
